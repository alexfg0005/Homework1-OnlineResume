Freshman Year E10 Class - Robotics Project

In my freshman year, I took an E10 class where we were tasked with building a robot designed to perform a set of challenges, such as finding a beacon and hitting a button. The project began with learning about basic robotics principles, including motor control, sensors, and programming techniques. Our first step was to design and assemble the robot using simple components, like motors for movement, sensors for detection, and a microcontroller to control everything. As a group, we discussed how to approach the tasks and made sure the robot could navigate its environment and complete the required actions autonomously.

Once the physical build was completed, we focused on programming the robot to carry out specific tasks. This phase involved coding the robot to search for the beacon using sensors, navigate around obstacles, and reach its target efficiently. The most difficult challenge was ensuring the robot could find the beacon and accurately hit the button without making mistakes. Through trial and error, we tested different approaches to fine-tune the robot's behavior, and after many adjustments, the robot was able to perform the tasks consistently and with precision.

After several rounds of testing and tweaking, the robot was ready for the final challenge. We successfully demonstrated the robot's ability to find the beacon and hit the button in front of the class. The project was a great learning experience, where I not only strengthened my problem-solving and programming skills but also worked closely with classmates to achieve our goals. The project's success highlighted the importance of teamwork, persistence, and learning from mistakes, and it gave us valuable hands-on experience that prepared us for more advanced robotics tasks in the future.
